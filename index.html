Introduction

Vision-Language Models have made significant progress on perceptual tasks, however, their progress on reasoning-focused tasks seem to be limited due to the lack of high-quality data. In this work, we aim to address the scarcity issue of reasoning-focused multimodal datasets. We propose VisualWebInstructâ€”a novel approach that leverages search engine to create a diverse, and high-quality dataset spanning multiple disciplines. Starting with 30,000 seed images across fields including Math, Physics, Accounting, Chemistry, etc. We employ Google Image search to identify websites containing similar images, collecting and processing 758,490 unique URL sources. Through a pipeline of content extraction, filtering and synthesis, we build a dataset of approximately 900K question-answer pairs, with 40% being visual QA pairs. Models fine-tuned on VisualWebInstruct demonstrate significant performance improvements: Llava-OV-mid shows 10-20% absolute point gains across benchmarks, while our MAmmoTH-VL2 model achieves state-of-the-art performance within the 10B parameter class on MMMU-Pro-std (40.7%), MathVerse (42.6%), and DynaMath (55.7%). These results highlight VisualWebInstruct's effectiveness in enhancing VLMs' reasoning capabilities for complex multimodal tasks.
